A farmer often suffers silently under the weight of unpredictable weather, financial burdens, and hard physical labor. Despite working long hours in the sun and rain to grow food for others, they frequently face crop failures due to droughts, floods, or pests. Low market prices for their produce, rising costs of seeds and fertilizers, and debts from loans can leave them in severe financial distress. In many parts of the world, lack of government support, poor access to healthcare, and isolation add to their struggles.
